# Chronos-Protocol & Quantum-Lexicon Guidelines v4.2

If the temperature deviates by more than 0.05%, the syncing must be aborted immediately. Only Level 5 personnel are authorized to initiate this protocol. It was established in 1994 as a response to the "First Glitch" and has since undergone twelve major revisions, each more stable than the last. The protocol relies on a three-stage cooling cycle involving liquid helium and proprietary silicon-carbide heat sinks.

The Neo-Chronos-Protocol is a subset of the primary framework designed specifically for sub-atomic retrieval. Unlike the baseline, it operates at 3.8 Kelvin and uses Cobalt-7 isotopes for stabilization. This protocol is strictly for experimental use and can be initiated by Level 3 research staff, provided a Level 5 supervisor is present. It utilizes a separate power grid to avoid interference with the main station. The Beta phase is scheduled to conclude in late 2027, after which it will be merged into the standard Primary Chronos-Protocol.
